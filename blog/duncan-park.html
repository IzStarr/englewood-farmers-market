History of Duncan Park

April 2026

Englewood's Best Park!

A Growing City with Deep Roots

Englewood's story begins in the late 1800s, when the area developed as a small settlement just south of Denver. With the arrival of railroads and nearby industry, the town quickly grew into a working-class hub. Over time, Englewood evolved from its industrial beginnings into a close-knit suburban community, known for its local businesses, schools, and neighborhood parks.

The Role of Community Spaces

As Englewood expanded through the 20th century, public parks became an important part of city planning. These spaces weren't just for recreation. They were gathering places where neighbors could connect, families could spend time outdoors, and community traditions could take root.

Duncan Park Today

Duncan Park stands as one of those cherished spaces. With its open green areas, shaded spots, and welcoming layout, the park serves as a natural meeting point for residents. It's the kind of place where kids play, friends catch up, and local events like farmers markets bring the community together.

Spend a little time there and you'll see why. The park offers a pavilion and picnic area that make it easy to gather and stay awhile, a playground for families, and a basketball court and multi-use field for everything from pickup games to casual afternoons outside. There are even horseshoe pits that add a bit of old-school charm, along with an off-leash dog area that's a favorite among local pet owners. With simple on-street parking and an easygoing layout, it's a place that feels accessible and welcoming to everyone.

A Perfect Home for the Farmers Market

Hosting a farmers market at Duncan Park continues a long tradition of community connection. Just as Englewood grew through local industry and collaboration, today's market celebrates local farmers, artisans, and small businesses. It's a modern extension of the same values that shaped the city. Supporting one another, sharing resources, and building something meaningful together.
